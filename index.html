If you take exactly 2 circles in one move, it stays your turn. The computer plays by the same rule.
Each turn, a die rolls 1–5. You can take at most that many circles that turn.
Some circles are armoured (red). They must be taken twice — first hit damages them (orange), second removes them.
Linked circle pairs — take one and the other is taken too. Beware the kill circle (black/red) — take it and you lose instantly.
Circles collapse downward like sand after removal. Unsupported circles fall — 50/50 left or right if both sides are open.
After each move, pick your leftmost or rightmost removed circle — it cascades upward, removing that same column position in every row above.

📝 Credits

All ideas for this game came from my friend Jup1t3r. He taught me this game while on a cruise, and I lost... a lot. I tried to keep playing the game, but still didn't know the pattern well enough. I finally in 2025 started to make the game in Python. As you can tell, this game is not in Python, and that's where I got some help. I had AI help me with a lot of the JS and had it teach me HTML to make this, and I'm so glad I did. I'm so glad I get to share this little bit of joy I've found with you.

This game is a Misere-Nim style game that takes strategy and pattern recognition to win. It has lots of different maps and different modes of AI to help you learn those patterns and embed them into your mind. After you get the patterns down, there are more for you to learn with 4 distinct gamemodes to pick from. You can even combine gamemodes to have even more fun. Beat your friends on the leaderboard and get achievements to show how good you are at the game.
